Question

Consider the following problem:

Island of Knights and Knaves. There are two types of inhabitants:

Knights always make true statements.

Knaves always make false statements.

Everyone on the island is either a Knight or a Knave.

There is a couple on the island and you ask them 'What type are you?'.

The husband responds 'We are both Knaves.'

What type is the husband and what type is the wife?

Answer

If the husband were a Knight, he would never have claimed that he and his wife were both Knaves.

Therefore he must be a Knave. Since he is a Knave, his statement is false; so they are not both Knaves.

This means his wife must be a Knight.

Therefore the husband is a Knave, and the wife is a Knight.

from Raymond Smullyan: 'Forever Undecided', pg 16
