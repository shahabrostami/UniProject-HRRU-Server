Question

A regular six-sided die has four green faces and two red faces.

The die will be rolled 20 times.

Which sequence is more likely to show up?

Answer

Because the die has twice as many green as red faces, the first sequence is quite unrepresentative.

The second sequence, which contains six tosses, is a better fit to what we would expect from this die, because it includes two G's.

However, this sequence was constructed by adding a G to the beginning of the first sequence, so it can only be less likely than the first.

Many people take their intuition as certainty without considering the other options - leading to false answers.

from Daniel Kahneman: 'Thinking Fast and Slow', pg 162
