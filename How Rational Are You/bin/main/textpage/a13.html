Question

In a lake, there is a patch of lily pads.

Every day the patch doubles in size.

If it takes 48 days for the patch to cover the entire lake, how long would it take for the patch to cover half of the lake?

Answer

This is a simple logic problem.

It takes a day for the patch to double in size.

Therefore a Day less than 48, would mean the patch would half in size.

This means that at Day 47 the patch covered half the lake, and this would double the next day, covering the whole lake - Day 48.

from Daniel Kahneman: 'Thinking Fast and Slow', pg 65
