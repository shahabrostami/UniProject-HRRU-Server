Question

You are given a choice of drawing a marble from one of three urns, in which red marbles win a prize:

Urn A: Contains 1000 marbles, of which 95 are red.

Urn B: Contains 100 marbles, of which 8 are red.

Urn C: Contains 10 marbles, of which 1 are red.

Which urn would have the highest chances of winning?

Answer

This is a simple logic problem.

The chances of winning are 9.5% in Urn A, 8% in Urn B, and 10% in Urn C, so making the right choice should be easy - Urn C, but it is not.

About 30-40% of students choose the Urn with the larger number of winning marbles, rather than the urn that provides a better chance of winning.

from Daniel Kahneman: 'Thinking Fast and Slow', pg 328
