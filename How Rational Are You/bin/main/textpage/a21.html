Question

Consider the following problem:

Island of Knights and Knaves. There are two types of inhabitants:

Knights always make true statements.

Knaves always make false statements.

Everyone on the island is either a knight or a knave.

There is a couple on the island and you ask them 'Are you both Knaves?'.

The husband responds 'At least one of us is.'

What type is the husband and what type is the wife?

Answer

If the husband were a Knave, then it would be true that at least one of the two is a Knave, hence a Knave would have made a true statement, which cannot be.

Therefore the husband must be a Knight. It then follows that his statement was true, which means that either he or his wife is a Knave. Since he isn't a Knave, then his wife is.

The husband is a Knight, and the wife is a Knave.

from Raymond Smullyan: 'Forever Undecided', pg 16
